Wenchao Ding (丁文超）

I am Wenchao Ding, a fifth-year Ph.D. student from the Electronic and Computer Engineering Department at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, where I am advised by Prof. Shaojie Shen and working in the HKUST Aerial Robotics Group.


My main research interest lies in planning. During my first year into robotics, I mainly worked on planning problems for macro aerial robots such as quadrotors. I loved developing methods which are elegantly formulated and at the same time have practical impacts.


After that, I devoted to the field of autonomous driving. My interests and work include behavior/trajectory prediction, motion planning, and decision making for autonomous vehicles. I am right now working on deploying my work on a real vehicle.

Past projects

• Decision-making for Autonomous Vehicles (NEW!)

An efficient uncertainty-aware decision-making framework which can work under imperfect tracking and inaccurate prediction. (ICRA 2020)

• Prediction for Autonomous Vehicles

1) Novel learning-based network to model and predict future behaviors of agent vehicles. The key feature is modeling the interaction among vehicles to enhance prediction accuracy and extend the prediction horizon. (ICRA 2019)

2) Two-level behavior & trajectory prediction framework: online hierarchical prediction framework for modeling multimodal behaviors and contextual factors in complex urban environments. (Also ICRA 2019)

• Motion planning for Autonomous Vehicles

A safe and unified motion planning framework for modeling numerous semantic elements in complex urban environments based on spatiotemporal information. The key feature is that it can work in complex urban environments and has a safety guarantee. (IEEE RA-L 2019)

• Planning for Micro Aerial Vehicles

Trajectory planning for monocular vision-based quadrotors: efficient trajectory replanning framework for onboard autonomous flight in unknown indoor and outdoor environments with only one camera and one IMU. (ICRA 2018 & IEEE Transactions on Robotics & JFR)
